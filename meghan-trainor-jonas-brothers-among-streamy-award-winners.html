The Streamy Awards were held on Sunday night, honoring the best in online video entertainment. Meghan Trainor and the Jonas Brothers were among the winners. Charli D’Amelio was also nominated for two awards, but she didn’t win.

Meghan Trainor, Jonas Brothers Win Streamy Awards

During Sunday’s show, Meghan Trainor was announced as the winner of the Rolling Stone Sound of the Year award for her song “Made You Look.” The singer looked excited to accept the award in a video from the ceremony.

“I wrote this for my postpartum body,” Trainor shared of the song, before mentioning her two children. “I just had a baby. Shout-out to my babies, Riley and Barry. Mama loves you!”

Trainor also thanked fans for making videos set to “Made You Look,” saying it “means so much to us songwriters.” The track went viral on social media platforms like TikTok when it was released last year.

Meanwhile, the Jonas Brothers walked away with this year’s Crossover award, which goes to a “public figure embracing digital culture and demonstrating best practices.” Dancing with the Stars winner Charli D’Amelio was also nominated for Creator of the Year and Lifestyle, but she missed out on both.

Trainor Gave Birth to Her Second Child in July

As Trainor mentioned in her Streamy Awards acceptance speech, she recently had a baby. The singer gave birth to her second child with husband Daryl Sabara in July. The couple had a boy, naming him Barry Bruce. He joins big brother Riley, who is 2.

Trainor recently told People that she’s “fully recovered” from her C-section, and baby Barry “is so good.” She added, “He’s so good to me. We got good sleeping babies, so he’s already sleeping in to like 5:30 at 2 months old.”

The singer said Sunday’s Streamy Awards was her first time “getting glam” since giving birth, and she was nervous to take the stage, sharing, “I’ve never fangirled more at an event than this one.”
