8 Underwear Rules That Will Help You in Hot Weather

Posted by Kelle Repass on Thursday, August 15, 2024

The summer of 2023 has been unusually hot. There are days when you don’t feel like going out, and all you can do is reach for the air conditioner remote. Here are some tips on how to choose the right lingerie that will make you more comfortable in the heat.

Wear hipsters with loose-fitting dresses.

If the dress is loose and flowy, you can wear almost any kind of underwear under it. You can opt for more modest styles, such as hipsters. If the wind blows, it won’t be a big deal. People won’t see too much of your bare skin.

Wear shorts to prevent your thighs from chafing.

You can find many options online for any taste and budget. These shorts can be made of pure cotton and feel very light on your skin. They are comfortable to wear under long dresses.

Choose light-colored underwear.

Light-colored underwear is better for hot weather. White reflects light, while darker shades absorb it. The sun’s rays can get through loose fabric, so light-colored underwear will keep you a bit cooler.

Also, if you can, choose cotton styles. For an outfit like Margot Robbie’s, it’s better to wear light underwear, because the dress fits snugly on the hips. This can make you feel warm, and dark underwear will make it worse.

You don’t have to wear underwear if you don’t want to.

Underwear is comfortable and hygienic. But if you wear a long skirt, you don’t need to wear underwear at all.

However, if you wear tight clothes, such as jeans, you should change your underwear more frequently. Sometimes — even several times a day. Gynecologists explain this by saying that in such clothes you sweat more, your underwear gets damp, so you should replace it.

Apply deodorant on more than just your armpits.

If your bra is digging in and chafing, the easiest thing to do is to throw it away and buy a new one. But before that, you can try to fix the issue. First of all, never wear a bra on a wet body, because the moisture can make it rub harder.

To prevent the bra from digging in, you can use deodorant not only under your arms but also in places where the bra touches, for example — near the clasp or on the straps. You can also use deodorant to stop your legs from rubbing on your thighs.

A strapless bra is a great option.

In summer, we wear open clothes with thin straps. If you don’t feel comfortable going braless, a strapless bra is perfect. It gives enough support but still lets you wear open tops.

For bigger breasts, this kind of bra will also do. Just pick options that have more hooks and a snug fit on your body.

You can wear elastic panties under tight dresses.

Some people don’t want to compromise their office style in summer. At work, you can wear the usual fitted dress, but with a thong. In such outfits, the outline of the underwear can show, so it’s better to choose seamless underwear. You can also look for models with a slight shaping effect in the waist, so that your clothes look flawless.

Opt for boxers with trousers

If the fabric is dense enough and doesn’t reveal anything, you can skip underwear altogether. Or pick underwear that matches the shape of your body, such as boxers, which have become trendy lately.
