Who is Al Roker?

Albert Lincoln Roker is a 65-year old American television personality who rose to stardom appearing as the anchor and weather forecaster on NBC's Today show. He was born in Queens, New York, to Isabel and Albert Lincoln senior, who was of Bahamian descent. Albert attended Xavier High School in Manhattan, where he dedicated himself to several projects involving cartoons and illustrations.

From there, the television personality went further to study at the State University of New York, Oswego branch. There, he majored in graphics communication. However, he took classes in meteorology in a bid to satisfy the science requirement of the university. Eventually, Albert found out that he had a talent for meteorology and his interest in science grew as the course progressed.

After he finished college, he got an opportunity of working at NBC at WKYC-TV in Cleveland. With dedication and passion committed to the work, he got a promotion after five years of working there. His new position granted him the opportunity of moving to his hometown, where he worked with WNBC-TV.

At first, he served as a weekend weathercaster. Then, eight months after, he became the regular weeknight weathercaster for the station. Eventually, he landed the opportunity of presenting as a weekday weather forecaster. Even though he did not hold a degree in meteorology, Albert became known for writing his weather forecasts using NBC's radar instead of the National weather service.
